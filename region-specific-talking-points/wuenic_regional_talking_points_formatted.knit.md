\textcolor{white}{\large \textbf{Overview}}
\end{tcolorbox}

\phantomsection\hypertarget{sec:overview}{}\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{Overview}

\vspace{1em}

-   While noting variations across countries and vaccines, none of the 15 childhood vaccines achieved an average coverage of 90% or higher within the ESAR region in 2025.

-   Coverage of the first dose of diphtheria-tetanus-pertussis-containing vaccine (DTP1) was the same in 2025 as it was in 2024 (85%), and the third dose of DTP-containing vaccine (DTP3) was the same in 2025 as it was in 2024 (79%). Coverage of the first dose of the vaccine against measles (MCV1) was the same in 2025 as it was in 2024 (76%).

-   At the country level, 12 out of 21 (57%) countries achieved coverage of 90% or higher in at least one of the 15 childhood vaccines administered in the region.
-   There were 9 countries that did not achieve at least 90% coverage in any vaccine (Angola, Burundi, Comoros, Ethiopia, Madagascar, Somalia, South Sudan, Tanzania, and South Africa).

-   In 2025, the number of zero-dose children (2.9m) was approximately 51% higher than the annual goal to achieve the Immunization Agenda 2030 target of reducing the number of zero-dose children by half by 2030 in ESAR region (1.9m).

\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=unicefblue, colframe=unicefblue, arc=0mm, sharp corners]
\textcolor{white}{\large \textbf{DTP1: Reaching zero-dose children}}
\end{tcolorbox}

\phantomsection\hypertarget{sec:dtp1}{}\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{DTP1: Reaching zero-dose children}

-   The number of children vaccinated with DTP1 increased 13% from 2.5m in 2019 to 2.9m in 2025.

-   In 2025, the number of zero-dose children (2.9m) was approximately 51% higher than the annual goal to achieve the Immunization Agenda 2030 target in ESAR region (1.9m).

-   With 1.5m more surviving infants in the region compared to in 2019, maintaining current coverage requires vaccinating an increasing number of children. To improve coverage and reach the 2030 ZD target will require substantial increases in immunization programme and health system capacity. 

-   In 2025, 2 countries, Ethiopia and Angola, represented approximately 27% and 14% of all zero-dose children in ESAR (n = 2.9m), respectively. Ethiopia had the highest number of zero-dose children at 772,000, and Angola had the second highest at 413,000. 

-   The lowest DTP1 coverage was observed in Angola and Madagascar at 70%, followed by South Africa and South Sudan at 76%. 

-   There were 10 (48%) out of 21 countries in the region that achieved DTP1 coverage of 90% or higher.\vspace{1em}

\phantomsection\hypertarget{sec:dtp3}{}\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{DTP3: A marker of routine immunization service delivery to children}

\vspace{1em}

-   The number of children vaccinated with DTP3 increased from 13.5m in 2019 to 14.6m in 2025. 

-   In 2025, 7% of children who received DTP1 did not receive DTP3. DTP drop-out was the same as in 2019. The medium DTP drop-out rates imply moderate ability to provide a complete series of vaccines early in life. 

-   In 2025, DTP3 coverage ranged from 60% in Madagascar to 98% in Rwanda. 

-   Ethiopia had DTP3 coverage of 73% and the highest absolute number of un- and undervaccinated children in the region (1.1m), followed by Angola with 64% coverage and 495,000 un- and undervaccinated children.

\end{tcolorbox}

\phantomsection\hypertarget{sec:mcv}{}\addcontentsline{toc}{section}{MCV: Canary in the coal mine}

\vspace{1em}

-   MCV1 coverage in 2025 (76%) remained relatively stable, within 1% of the 2019 level (76%). 

-   MCV2 coverage increased from 46% in 2019 to 60% in 2025. This still leaves 4.5m children without any protection and another 2.6m with only partial protection against the measles virus. 

-   In 2025, 10% of children who received DTP1 did not receive MCV1. DTP-MCV drop-out was 1 percentage points lower than in 2019 (11%). The medium DTP-MCV drop-out rates imply moderate retention in immunization programmes and ability to provide a full course of vaccines in infancy (up to one year).

-   Over the last 3 years, 7 countries (Angola, Comoros, Ethiopia, Madagascar, Mozambique, Somalia, and South Sudan) consistently had MCV1 coverage below 80%.

-   Ethiopia had the highest number of children who did not receive the measles vaccine (1.1m), representing approximately 26% of all children unprotected against measles in ESAR (4.5m).

\textbf{Categorization Logic:} This analysis tracks DTP3 coverage trends from 2019 to 2025 to assess post-pandemic recovery. 
Countries are categorized based on their progress toward 2019 baseline levels:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Recovered:} 2024 coverage is equal to or higher than 2019 levels.
    \item \textbf{Recovering:} Coverage is still below 2019 levels but has improved by at least 3 percentage points from its lowest point during the pandemic.
    \item \textbf{Not Recovered:} Coverage remains below 2019 levels and has shown less than 3 percentage points of improvement from the pandemic low.
\end{itemize}

\textcolor{cyan}{\large WHO/UNICEF Estimates of National Immunization Coverage (WUENIC)}

\vspace{1em}

Each year, WHO and UNICEF review national immunization data from Member States, including administrative and official coverage, survey estimates and contextual information, and use rules-based data triangulation approach to assess coverage. Estimates are produced independently, without borrowing data from other countries, and are not based on ad hoc adjustments. In some cases, they rely on a single source, typically nationally reported coverage. When data for a specific country-vaccine-year are unavailable, values from adjacent years are interpolated or extrapolated, and conflicting sources are assessed to determine the most reliable estimate, considering potential biases and expert opinion.
